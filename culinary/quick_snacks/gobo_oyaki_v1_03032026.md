# Gobo Oyaki or Gobo-yaki; Grated Burdock Pancake #

Since the pancake (often called Gobo Oyaki or Gobo-yaki) is the winner, here is a streamlined way to make them extra satisfying. Because your burdock is already grated and frozen, it will soften beautifully into the batter.
The "Crispy-Chewy" Ratio
To get that perfect texture, use a mix of flour for structure and potato starch (or cornstarch) for the crisp edges.
| Ingredient | Amount (Approx.) |
|---|---|
| Grated Burdock | 1 cup (squeezed dry) |
| Flour | 2 tbsp |
| Potato/Corn Starch | 1 tbsp |
| Egg | 1 (acts as the binder) |
| Seasoning | A pinch of salt or 1 tsp soy sauce |
Quick Steps for Success
 * Thaw & Squeeze: Place the frozen grated burdock in a fine-mesh strainer and run warm water over it for 30 seconds. Squeeze it hard with your hands to remove excess moisture—this is the secret to a pancake that isn't soggy.
 * Mix: Toss the burdock with the dry ingredients first so every strand is coated, then stir in the egg.
 * The "Lacy" Fry: Heat a thin layer of oil in a skillet. Drop small spoonfuls and press them thin with your spatula.
 * The Flip: Fry for about 2–3 minutes per side until the edges look like golden lace.
3-Second Flavor Boosters
If you want to level up the flavor without extra prep, toss one of these into the batter:
 * Toasted Sesame Seeds: Adds a deep nuttiness that pairs perfectly with burdock.
 * Shredded Mozzarella: Creates a "cheesy pull" inside the chewy pancake.
 * Chopped Scallions: For a fresh, sharp bite.
> Pro-Tip: If you have some honey and soy sauce on hand, mix equal parts and brush it onto the pancakes while they are still hot in the pan. The sugar will caramelize instantly and create a sticky, savory glaze.
