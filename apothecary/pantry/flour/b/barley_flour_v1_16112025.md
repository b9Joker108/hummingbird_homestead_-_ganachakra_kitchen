What did the Himalayan people use traditionally to make torma as grain or flour was exceptional and scarce?

Direct answer: Traditionally, Himalayan peoples made torma (ritual cakes) from barley flour (tsampa) and yak butter, because barley was the most reliable grain at altitude. When grain was scarce, they substituted or supplemented with butter, curd, and sometimes clay or permanent materials to maintain the ritual form without consuming precious food supplies.
