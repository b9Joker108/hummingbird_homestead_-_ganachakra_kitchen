**Quenelle?**

A quenelle is a smooth, oval-shaped scoop of soft food, traditionally formed with two spoons. In classical French cuisine, it originally referred to a delicate dumpling made from fish, meat, or poultry bound with egg and poached. Today, the term is often used more broadly to describe the shape rather than the recipe ‚Äî for example, a quenelle of ice cream, mashed potato, or even funchi.  

---

üîé Key Points About Quenelles
- Origin: The word comes from the German Kn√∂del (dumpling). In 18th‚Äì19th century French haute cuisine, quenelles were refined dumplings, often of pike, served with rich sauces.  
- Shape: The hallmark is the rugby-ball / oval form, created by passing a soft mixture between two spoons until smooth.  
- Technique:  
  1. Dip two spoons in hot water.  
  2. Scoop a portion of the mixture with one spoon.  
  3. Pass it back and forth between the spoons, smoothing the sides into an oval.  
- Modern usage: In fine dining, chefs use the quenelle shape to give elegance to foods that would otherwise look rustic ‚Äî ice cream, sorbet, mousse, mashed vegetables or polenta.  

---

üçΩ Why It Matters for Your Dish
